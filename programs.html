Sponsorship to pre-school units

This is when our sponsored children are at their tender age, before they join primary school. At this stage we them to daycare-centres/pre-schools and we pay the costs of the services they get while being at the centres. This is usually when the children are between 2 and 5 years old. Currently, almost all of our our pre-school sponsored kids go to KHM/Unique Choice Daycare Centre.

When they are at the pre-school(s), they get all necessary services for a little child. The services include:

- Learn basic social skills

- Get introduced to basic academic concepts

- Get porridge and lunch at school

- Get cleaned after toilet

- Access to first aid services

- And full time security and supervision

Our Friend Family Program

In Tanzania’s urban areas, there are many cases where people find themselves in such a situation that they are being kicked out of a house that has been their home for a long time, just because they have failed to pay rent. In most cases, according to the laws of Tanzania, the landlord/lady would give them one – three months of notice wanting them to leave. When the time is ripe, the landlord is entitled to take whatever action towards the tenant, including kicking them out of the house. This is when our program comes into play.

We help people in this situation get a home for at least two years before they can be independent and stand on their own feet. We know that there are so many reasons leading to those situations. One is when a man abandons his family because of failure of providing basic needs to the family. In most cases, the woman is left with the children not knowing what to do and where to go.

This situation is more difficult if the woman was previously made a mere house wife. Their money earning capacity is reduce to minimum because of such circumstances. This makes it difficult for them to provide for their family when the man leaves. Many women end up being prostitutes, while their children take to the street and turning into street children.

Friend Family Program - Details

How it works?

When we get case like this, we usually pay a visit to their current home where their being evacuated. We evaluate the overall situation and come up with a full narration of the situation on paper. Where possible, we ask to meet the landlord/lady to ask for grace period, if our request is accepted then we use the grace period to look for a sponsor who can pay rent for this poor family. We do this by sending the information to our Family Friend program patron/patroness. After receiving the information, our Family Friend program patron/patroness shares the news with their respective networks. When a sponsor is found our Family Friend program patron/patroness inform us, and we immediately inform the waiting family about the attainment of a sponsor. The sponsor is expected to pay 30 Euros every month for 3 years.

Just after identifying the family we invite the head of the family to our business training sessions, happening once every week. In this training they learn a lot of business concepts. What is a business, how to develop a business idea, market research, customer care and how to tackle competition in business. The continue doing this training even after getting a sponsor. This is to prepare them to start their own business as way of eventually becoming independent. In one and a half or two years time we want them to come up with a specific and realistic business plan. We expect them to use the knowledge that they will have learned during the business training sessions. When their business plans is evaluate to good, a process to find them capital starts. There are two major ways to get them business capital one is through a donation by a onetime donor just for that business idea turn into reality. The other way is by using one Take a Malaika microcredit scheme. If they qualify for a loan they then get one.

This time we take a responsibility of making sure the business stands stable. We offer the client advice and from time to time we pay them a visit to see how everything is going with the business. After two or two and half years we tell them that they should now be independent. This is end of our support to that family.

The volunteer program

Our volunteer program is one of the most important programs in our organisation. Volunteers have always been a big part of our acheivements. Volunteering with us is a great support to us and a centre of knowledge exchange to both, whereby the volunteer also gains some experience. You can come and volunteer with us directly or come through our partner organisations.

Our partner organisations offer very good volunteer services. You can sign up with them to reach your volunteering goals as well as supporting a community. Step-Africa and VIVA Tanzania are our reliable partner organisations who provide affordable and quality volunteer services. You can read more about them here.

For those who would like to come to us directly, you are most welcome! We receive volunteers from anywhere around the world, as long as they meet volunteer placement requirements. We can accommodate volunteers throughout the year. With this program, we have a number of areas where people who are willing to work with us can engage in.

The volunteer program - details

Volunteer at KHM/Unique Choice Daycare Centres

Here you have an opportunity to do the following:

  • Teach children between the age of 2 to the age of 5
  • Exchange ideas with local teachers, during the teachers meetings, on how to improve teaching
  • play with the children and teach them sports and games
  • Help with the office management of the Centre
  • Help the centre with their social media updating
  • Help with serving food to the children
  • Home visits to potential sponsored children's homes
  • Fundrase for purpose of improving services provided

Work on the women's program

In this program, our potential volunteers are expected to accomplish the following:

  • Help us with our long time plan to develop a small microfinancing program (Nyongeza Fund)
  • Help us with proposal writing for the same purpose of Making Nyongeza Fund possible
  • Help us with Fundrasing for the Nyongeza Fund program
  • Link us with potential donor organisations and help us meet the requirements
  • Teach women basic business skills during their regular meetings at our Community Cenre
  • Home visits to the homes of potential beneficiaries of our Nyongeza Fund Scheme
  • Attend staff meetings so that to share ideas with the rest of the team on general issues
  • Train women on general subjects like women's rights, health issues and general life-skills

Work on the youth program

This is another area where our potential volunteers can engage in. We expect the volunteer to provide life-skills education to the youth in our targeted areas. The subjects that are in demand are:

  • Time management
  • Communication skills
  • Handling emotions
  • Problem solving
  • Reproductive health
  • Sexual Relationships
  • Sports and games
  • Personal hygiene
  • HIV/AIDS education
  • Environmental conservation education
  • Basic Business skills
